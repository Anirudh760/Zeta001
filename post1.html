ENLIGHTENMENT

Let's have a little background so that it can be understood beautifully. My parents, like many others, sought peace and spirituality in their lives. They were blessed with OSHODHARA, and naturally, I started going there at an early age.

As a child, I saw the enlightened gurus and thought they had some kind of mystical power—like reading minds or knowing everything. As I grew older and understood the world, I realized that it may or may not be true.

“Enlightenment is not an achievement; it is a realization. It is a journey inward.”

Many of us believe enlightenment is an end product, a final treasure waiting at the end of the road. But that's a misconception. We've been conditioned to think that way—finish school, get a degree, get a job, and then you'll be 'free.'

But true enlightenment is not something you acquire at the end of a journey. It is the journey itself. Through meditation, you dive deep within yourself. When you reach the end, you realize you already found it along the way. It’s that moment of click—a sudden understanding that this was it all along.

This is my perspective for now. Maybe in the future, my view will change. Time will tell.
